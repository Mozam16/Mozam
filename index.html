ETHNIC GROUPS IN NIGERIA

YORUBA

The Yoruba people are one of the largest ethnic groups in Nigeria with a population of around 40 million,primarily located in the southwestern region of the country.They have a rich culture and history,with a complex system of beliefs and traditions.They are known for their art and music,and have had a sinificant impact on Nigerian and African culture.There are six states in Nigeria that are predominantly unhabited by the Yoruba ethnic group; Lagos, Ogun, Osun, Oyo and Ekiti.The Yoruba people have also been involved in many political and social movement through out Nigeria's history.

IGBO

The Igbo people are one of the largest ethnic groups in Nigeria,primarily located in the southeastern region of the country.They are known for their rich culture and traditions,including their music ,dance and festivals.The Igbo people have a strong sense of community and family ties,and have been involved in many political and social movements throughout Nigeria's history.There are five states in Nigeria that are predominantly unhabited by the Igbo ethnic group; Abia, Anambra, Ebonyi, Enugu and Imo.They also have a strong enterprebeurial spirit and have made a sinificant contribution to Nigeria's economy.

HAUSA

The Hausa people are one of the largest ethnic groups in Nigeria,primarily located in the northern region of the country,with a population of over 70 million.They are known for their trading,leatherwork and agricultural skills.The Hausa people have a rich cultural heritage,including traditional music, dance and clothing.There are nineteen states in Nigeria that are predominantly unhabited by the Hausa ethnic group;Jigawa, Kaduna,Kano,Kastina,Kebbi,Sokoto,Zamfara,Bauchi,Borno,Gombe,Adamawa,Taraba,Yobe,Benue,Kwara,Niger,Plateau,Nasarawa and the Federal Capital Territory(Abuja)
